ADDIE BUNDREN

Files

Physical:

- Middle aged, average build

- Questionable mental health, no medical records

- No record of previous criminal acts

Pertinent Information:

Exhibit A:

- “And now he has three children that are his and that are not mine. And then I could get ready to die.” (176)
- It's not hard to spawn nihilistic and miserable outlooks on life, not only for women, but for everyone back then because of the constant hardship everyone faced.

Exhibit B:

- “Then I believed I would kill Anse. It was as though he had tricked me, hidden within a word like within a paper screen, and struck me in the back through it.” (172)
- It's extremely easy for women to yearn for more and to not receive any, nor would they even have any say on the matter, given how socially immobile women were back then.

Alibi:

- Given my nihilism, I might have had a suspected motive if the cause of death was suicide, but it wasn't, so this leaves me in an open-ended spot as a suspect.

Final Verdict?

- In general, being a mother and a woman was hard back then, when women weren't allowed to do anything and were confined to very strict roles. The fact that I hate it all is what I think Faulkner was designing his commentary about - using me as a juxtaposition to the world around me to give the readers a link into the world.
