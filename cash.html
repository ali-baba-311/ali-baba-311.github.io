Pertinent Information:

Exhibit A:

- Working men like Cash largely need to focus on his job and his job alone, with not much time or resources to do anything else due to constantly being short on money, in our case.

Exhibit B:

- “ ‘How far’d you fall, Cash?’ ‘Twenty eight foot, four and a half inches, about,’ Cash says. [...] ‘It’s too bad,’ I say. ‘But you couldn’t a holp it.’ ” (90)
- It's very dangerous to do some jobs, and even more so with no safety considerations and primitive technology back then, so working men like Cash are always getting hurt with no compensation.

Alibi:

- Cash never paid much mind to me, or did much other than work, even if his tools could probably kill me. No evidence or suspected motives here.

Final Verdict?

- Because of the lack of technology, Cash and others are put through horrible hours, always desperate for work, and almost always endangered on the job. Cash is never shown doing anything other than existing or building my coffin in the book, and I'd probably feel bad about it if I wasn't dead.
