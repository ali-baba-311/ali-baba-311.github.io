Case #1897

Victim: Addie Bundren

Cause of Death: Debatable

Date of Death: circa 1928

Background:

In As I Lay Dying by William Faulkner, the character of Addie, while slowly built up over the

course of the novel as a traditional mother and responsible figure, is quickly revealed to be

nihilistic and sadistic, as well as a stark juxtaposition to every other character in the novel.

Addie's character serves as Faulkner's focal point in a greater discussion throughout the novel

about the difficulties of living in the countryside in 20th century Southern life.
